The Mystery of Oud

Elena Vance Jan 25, 2026 3 Comments

Oud, also known as agarwood, is often referred to as "liquid gold" in the world of perfumery. But what exactly is it, and why does it command such reverence?

The story of oud begins with the Aquilaria tree. When the heartwood of this tree becomes infected with a specific type of mold, the tree produces a dark, aromatic resin to protect itself. This resin-embedded wood is what we know as oud.

A Scent Like No Other

The profile of oud is incredibly complex. It can be sweet and woody, or animalic and earthy. It brings a profound depth and longevity to any fragrance it touches. At Perfume Atelier, we source our oud sustainably from plantations in Southeast Asia that ensure the longevity of the Aquilaria species.

"Oud is not just an ingredient; it is a character. It demands attention and narrates a story of ancient woods and sacred rituals."

Blending with oud requires a delicate hand. We often pair it with Turkish Rose to soften its edges, or with bright citrus notes like Bergamot to create a striking contrast.
